# HRSA Health Center Compliance Requirements Summary

## Overview
This document summarizes the compliance requirements extracted from the HRSA Health Center Program Compliance Manual. The system analyzes health center applications against these 9 key compliance areas.

---

## 1. Needs Assessment (Chapter 3)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(2) and Section 330(k)(3)(J) of the PHS Act; 42 CFR 51c.104(b)(2-3), 42 CFR 51c.303(k), 42 CFR 56.104(b)(2), 42 CFR 56.104(b)(4), and 42 CFR 56.303(k)

### Key Requirement
**Element b - Update of Needs Assessment**

The health center must complete or update a needs assessment at least once every three years to inform and improve service delivery.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Frequency and Use of Needs Assessment**
- Conduct or update needs assessment at least every 3 years
- Use results to inform and improve service delivery
- Document how findings are applied to operations

**b) Data Sources and Service Area Analysis**
- Use most recently available data for service area
- Include special populations if applicable (MHC, HCH, PHPC)
- Cite data sources where applicable

**c) Required Analysis Areas**

*Access to Care Factors:*
- Geography and transportation barriers
- Occupation and employment status
- Transience and housing instability
- Unemployment rates
- Income levels
- Educational attainment

*Health Status Indicators:*
- Most significant causes of morbidity (e.g., diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer, low birth weight, substance use disorder)
- Most significant causes of mortality
- Associated health disparities by population group

*Unique Health Care Needs:*
- Social risk factors (poverty, food insecurity, violence)
- Physical environment factors (pollution, housing quality)
- Cultural and ethnic factors affecting care
- Language needs and barriers
- Housing status (homelessness, unstable housing)

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Need section, items 2a-c

**Notes:**
- May use data from Service Area Competition (SAC) or Renewal of Designation (RD) application
- If direct data unavailable, may use extrapolation techniques for comparable populations
- Use best available data when extrapolation not feasible

## 2. Sliding Fee Discount Program (Chapter 9)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(G) of the PHS Act; 42 CFR 51c.303(f), 42 CFR 51c.303(g), 42 CFR 51c.303(u), 42 CFR 56.303(f), 42 CFR 56.303(g), and 42 CFR 56.303(u)

### Key Requirements

**Element b - Sliding Fee Discount Program Policies**

Board-approved policies must apply uniformly to all patients and address:

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Definitions**
- Clear definition of "income" (earnings over given period, excluding assets)
- Clear definition of "family" (household unit)
- Criteria for inclusions and exclusions in income calculation

**b) Patient Assessment Process**
- Assess ALL patients for sliding fee discount eligibility
- Assessment based ONLY on income and family size (no other factors)
- Document methods for making eligibility assessments
- Uniform application to all patients

**c) Sliding Fee Discount Schedule Structure**
- Ensure patient charges are adjusted based on ability to pay
- Structure must be consistent with board-approved policy
- Gradations in income levels clearly defined

**d) Nominal Charge Policy (if applicable)**
- Only for patients at or below 100% FPG
- Must be flat charge (not variable)
- Set at level that is nominal from patient's perspective (based on patient input, surveys, advisory committees)
- Must NOT reflect actual cost of service
- Must be less than fee paid by first sliding fee discount pay class above 100% FPG

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Response section, items 5a-d

**Notes:**
- Nominal charges are NOT "minimum fees," "minimum charges," or "co-pays"
- Income is distinguished from assets (assets are fixed resources, income is earnings)

---

**Element c - Sliding Fee for Column I Services**

For services provided directly by the health center (Form 5A: Services Provided, Column I):

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Full Discount (At or Below 100% FPG)**
- Provide full discount (100% discount) for individuals/families with annual income at or below 100% FPG
- Exception: May elect to have nominal charge (must meet Element b(d) criteria)
- If nominal charge used, must be less than first pay class above 100% FPG

**b) Partial Discounts (101-200% FPG)**
- Provide partial discounts for individuals/families with income above 100% and at or below 200% FPG
- Must include at least THREE (3) discount pay classes
- Discounts must adjust based on gradations in income levels
- Example: 101-125% FPG, 126-150% FPG, 151-175% FPG, 176-200% FPG (4 pay classes)

**c) No Discounts (Above 200% FPG)**
- No discounts provided for individuals/families with annual income above 200% FPG
- Charged full fee/rate

**Application Location:** Attachment 10: Sliding Fee Discount Schedule(s)

**Notes:**
- Structure must be consistent with board-approved policy
- If health center has access to other grants/subsidies supporting patient care, see Chapter 16: Billing and Collections

---

**Element e - Incorporation of Current Federal Poverty Guidelines**

The SFDS must incorporate the most recent Federal Poverty Guidelines (FPG).

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Current FPG Integration**
- Use most recently published FPG from HHS
- Update SFDS when new FPG is published (typically annually)
- All income thresholds based on current FPG percentages

**Application Location:** Attachment 10: Sliding Fee Discount Schedule(s) (Attachment 9 for look-alikes)

## 3. Key Management Staff (Chapter 11)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(I)(i) of the PHS Act; 42 CFR 51c.104(b)(4), 42 CFR 51c.303(p), 42 CFR 56.104(b)(5), and 42 CFR 56.303(p); and 45 CFR 75.308(c)(1)(ii)(iii)

### Key Requirements

**Element b - Documentation of Key Management Staff Positions**

The health center must document qualifications and responsibilities for each key management staff position.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Training Qualifications**
- Document required education/degrees
- Document required certifications or licenses
- Document required training programs or courses

**b) Experience Qualifications**
- Document years of experience required
- Document type of relevant experience needed
- Document specific competencies or skills

**c) Duties and Functions**
- Document specific responsibilities of the position
- Document reporting relationships
- Document scope of authority and decision-making
- Document key performance expectations

**Application Location:** Attachment 4: Position Descriptions for Key Management Staff

---

**Element d - CEO Responsibilities**

The Project Director/CEO must meet specific employment and oversight requirements.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Direct Employment**
- CEO must be directly employed by the health center
- For public agencies: Public agency (as awardee/designee) must directly employ CEO
- Cannot be contractor or consultant
- Must have formal employment relationship

**b) Board Reporting**
- CEO reports directly to the governing board
- Board has authority to select and dismiss CEO
- Regular reporting on health center operations
- Accountable to board for performance

**c) Oversight of Key Management Staff**
- Responsible for overseeing other key management staff
- Directs day-to-day activities of management team
- Ensures coordination across departments
- Manages performance of key staff

**d) Scope of Project Implementation**
- Carries out day-to-day activities necessary to fulfill HRSA-approved scope of project
- Ensures compliance with program requirements
- Implements board-approved policies and decisions
- Manages operations within approved budget

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Resources/Capabilities section, item 3b; Form 2: Staffing Profile; Attachment 3: Project Organizational Chart; Attachment 4: Position Descriptions

**Notes:**
- Public agencies with co-applicant structure: Public agency must directly employ CEO
- Board selection/dismissal of CEO is part of board oversight (see Chapter 19: Board Authority)

## 4. Contracts and Subawards (Chapter 12)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(I) and Section 330(q) of the PHS Act; 42 CFR 51c.113, 42 CFR 56.114, 42 CFR 51c.303(t), and 42 CFR 56.303(t); 45 CFR Part 75 Subpart D; Section 1861(aa)(4)(A)(ii) and Section 1905(l)(2)(B)(ii) of the Social Security Act

### Key Requirements

**Element e - HRSA Approval for Contracting Substantive Programmatic Work**

If the health center contracts for substantive programmatic work, prior HRSA approval is required.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Prior Approval Documentation**

Must be documented by ONE of the following:
- Approved competing continuation/renewal of designation application that included the arrangement, OR
- Approved post-award request (e.g., change in scope) submitted within project period

**b) Substantive Programmatic Work Definition**
- Work that is central to the HRSA-approved scope of project
- Services that directly support health center operations
- Activities that are core to meeting program requirements

**Application Location:** Form 8: Health Center Agreements

**Notes:**
- Select 'N/A' if Form 8 indicates no contracting for substantive programmatic work

---

**Element f - Required Contract Provisions**

All contracts supporting the HRSA-approved scope of project must include specific provisions.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Scope of Work**
- Specific activities or services to be performed clearly defined
- Goods to be provided clearly specified
- Deliverables and timelines documented
- Performance standards established

**b) Monitoring Mechanisms**
- Health center's methods for monitoring contractor performance
- Reporting requirements from contractor
- Performance evaluation criteria
- Quality assurance processes

**c) Data and Reporting Requirements**
- Contractor must provide data necessary for Federal financial reporting
- Contractor must provide data necessary for Federal programmatic reporting
- Record retention requirements specified
- Record access provisions for audits
- Audit cooperation requirements
- Property management provisions

**Application Location:** Form 8: Health Center Agreements

**Notes:**
- See HHS Grants Policy Statement for detailed guidance: https://www.hrsa.gov/grants/hhsgrantspolicy.pdf

---

**Element g - HRSA Approval to Subaward (Not Applicable for Look-alikes)**

If the health center makes subawards, prior HRSA approval is required.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Prior Approval Documentation**

Must be documented by ONE of the following:
- Approved competing continuation/renewal of designation application that included subrecipient arrangement, OR
- Approved post-award request (e.g., change in scope) submitted within project period

**Application Location:** Form 8: Health Center Agreements

**Notes:**
- Select 'N/A' if Form 8 indicates no subawards
- Not applicable for look-alike health centers

---

**Element h - Subaward Agreement (Not Applicable for Look-alikes)**

All subawards supporting the HRSA-approved scope of project must include specific provisions.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Scope of Project Delegation**
- Specific portion of HRSA-approved scope of project to be performed by subrecipient
- Clear delineation of subrecipient responsibilities
- Relationship to overall health center scope

**b) Program Requirements**
- Applicability of ALL Health Center Program requirements to subrecipient
- Subrecipient must comply with same standards as health center
- Subrecipient subject to program oversight

**c) Other Federal Program Requirements**
- Applicability of distinct statutory requirements of other Federal programs
- Applicability of regulatory requirements of associated programs
- Applicability of policy requirements related to scope of project

**d) Monitoring and Compliance**
- Health center's mechanisms to monitor subrecipient compliance
- Health center's mechanisms to monitor subrecipient performance
- Reporting requirements from subrecipient
- Corrective action procedures

**e) Data and Reporting Requirements**
- Subrecipient must provide data for Federal financial reporting
- Subrecipient must provide data for Federal programmatic reporting
- Record retention requirements
- Record access provisions for audits
- Audit cooperation requirements
- Property management provisions

**f) Cost Allowability**
- All costs paid by Federal subaward must be allowable under Federal Cost Principles (45 CFR 75 Subpart E)
- Documentation of cost reasonableness
- Compliance with cost allocation requirements

**Application Location:** Form 8: Health Center Agreements

**Notes:**
- Select 'N/A' if Form 8 indicates no subawards
- Not applicable for look-alike health centers
- Subrecipients generally eligible for FQHC payment rates, 340B, and FTCA (but require additional approvals)
- See HHS Grants Policy Statement for detailed guidance

## 5. Collaborative Relationships (Chapter 14)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(B) of the PHS Act; and 42 CFR 51c.303(n), 42 CFR 56.303(n), and 42 CFR 51c.305(h)

### Key Requirements

**Element a - Coordination and Integration of Activities**

The health center must document collaboration efforts to provide access to services not available through the health center.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Continuity of Care Across Community Providers**
- Establish referral relationships with specialists
- Coordinate care transitions between providers
- Share patient information (with consent) for care coordination
- Follow-up on referrals and consultations
- Track patient outcomes across care settings

**b) Access to Other Health or Community Services**
- Collaborate with social service organizations
- Connect patients to housing assistance
- Link patients to food security programs
- Coordinate with mental health and substance abuse services
- Partner with organizations serving special populations
- Facilitate access to transportation services
- Connect to employment and education resources

**c) Reduction in Non-Urgent Use of Hospital Emergency Departments**
- Collaborate with local hospitals
- Provide extended hours or urgent care services
- Establish protocols for ED diversion
- Educate patients on appropriate use of emergency services
- Provide after-hours nurse triage lines
- Track and reduce ED utilization rates

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Collaboration section, items 1a-c; Attachment 9: Collaboration Documentation (Attachment 8 for look-alikes)

---

**Element b - Collaboration with Other Primary Care Providers**

The health center must document coordination with other health services delivery projects serving similar populations.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Federally-Funded Programs**
- Coordinate with other Health Center Program award recipients in service area
- Coordinate with other Health Center Program look-alikes in service area
- Collaborate with other federally-funded health programs (e.g., Ryan White, IHS)
- Document formal and informal relationships

**b) State and Local Programs**
- Coordinate with state health department programs
- Collaborate with local health department initiatives
- Partner with county or municipal health services
- Engage with state-funded community health programs

**c) Minimum Requirement**
- At minimum, establish and maintain relationships with other health centers in service area
- If no collaboration documentation provided, explain why and provide documentation of request

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Collaboration section, item 2; Attachment 9: Collaboration Documentation (Attachment 8 for look-alikes)

## 6. Billing and Collections (Chapter 16)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(E), (F), and (G) of the PHS Act; and 42 CFR 51c.303(e), (f), and (g) and 42 CFR 56.303(e), (f), and (g)

### Key Requirements

**Element c - Participation in Insurance Programs**

The health center must participate in public and private insurance programs to maximize revenue.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Required Public Programs**
- **Medicaid:** Enrolled as Medicaid provider, billing for eligible patients
- **CHIP:** Enrolled as CHIP provider, billing for eligible children
- **Medicare:** Enrolled as Medicare provider, billing for eligible patients

**b) Other Appropriate Programs**
- Private health insurance (commercial plans)
- State-specific assistance programs
- Other public assistance programs as applicable
- Marketplace/Exchange plans

**c) Participation Requirements**
- Active enrollment and credentialing
- Regular billing and claims submission
- Compliance with program requirements
- Documentation of participation status

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Resources/Capabilities section, item 5b; Form 3: Income Analysis

---

**Element h - Policies or Procedures for Waiving or Reducing Fees**

The health center must have board-approved policies for fee waivers/reductions based on inability to pay.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Board-Approved Policies**
- Formal board approval of fee waiver/reduction policies
- Documented in board minutes or resolutions
- Regular review and updates as needed

**b) Specific Circumstances**
- Clear criteria for when fees will be waived
- Clear criteria for when fees will be reduced
- Income thresholds or guidelines
- Documentation requirements from patients
- Process for determining inability to pay

**c) Operating Procedures**
- Staff training on policies
- Procedures for assessing patient financial situation
- Documentation requirements for waivers/reductions
- Approval process and authority levels
- Patient notification procedures

**d) Utilization**
- Policies must be actively used, not just documented
- Regular application to eligible patients
- Tracking of waivers/reductions granted

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Resources/Capabilities section, item 5a

---

## 7. Budget (Chapter 17)

### Authority
Section 330(e)(5)(A) and Section 330(k)(3)(I)(i) of the PHS Act; and 45 CFR 75.308(a) and 45 CFR 75 Subpart E

### Key Requirements

**Element a - Budgeting for Scope of Project**

The health center must develop and submit an annual "total budget" reflecting all costs and revenues.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Total Budget Definition**
- Includes Health Center Program Federal award funds
- Includes ALL other sources of revenue supporting scope of project
- Comprehensive view of health center finances

**b) Projected Costs**
- Personnel costs (salaries, benefits)
- Operating expenses
- Equipment and supplies
- Facilities costs
- Contracted services
- All costs necessary to support HRSA-approved scope of project

**c) Projected Revenues**
- All anticipated revenue sources
- Realistic projections based on historical data
- Documentation of assumptions

**d) Submission Requirements**
- Submit to HRSA for new funding
- Submit for continued funding
- Submit for continued designation
- Annual updates as required

**Application Location:** SF-424A; Budget Narrative; Form 2: Staffing Profile

**Notes:**
- Requirements related to use of Federal award funds not applicable to look-alikes

---

**Element b - Revenue Sources**

The annual budget must include all projected revenue sources beyond the Health Center Program award.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Service-Generated Revenue**
- Patient fees for services
- Insurance premiums (if applicable)
- Third-party reimbursements:
  - Medicaid payments
  - Medicare payments
  - CHIP payments
  - Private insurance payments
  - Other payer reimbursements

**b) Government Grants and Contracts**
- State grants supporting health center operations
- Local government grants
- Other Federal grants:
  - Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program
  - Healthy Start
  - SAMHSA grants
  - Other HHS grants
  - Non-HHS Federal grants
- Government contracts for services

**c) Private Support**
- Foundation grants
- Corporate donations
- Individual contributions
- Fundraising proceeds
- In-kind donations (valued appropriately)

**d) Other Funding**
- Investment income
- Rental income
- Pharmacy revenue (340B)
- Any other funding supporting the Health Center Program project

**Application Location:** SF-424A; Budget Narrative; Form 2: Staffing Profile; Form 3: Income Analysis

## 8. Board Authority (Chapter 19)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(H) of the PHS Act; 42 CFR 51c.303(i), 42 CFR 56.303(i), 42 CFR 51c.304(d), and 42 CFR 56.304(d); and 45 CFR 75.507(b)(2)

### Key Requirements

**Element a - Maintenance of Board Authority Over Health Center Project**

Organizational structure and governing documents must ensure the board maintains full authority over the Health Center Program project.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) No External Approval Authority or Veto Power**
- No individual can reserve approval authority over the board
- No entity can have veto power over board decisions
- No committee (including executive committees) can override board decisions
- Executive committees may only act in emergencies (subject to full board ratification)
- Organizational documents must clearly establish board supremacy

**b) Collaboration Agreements**
- Agreements with other entities cannot restrict board's required authorities
- Agreements cannot infringe upon board's required functions
- Board retains final decision-making authority
- Partnerships must respect board governance role

**c) Public Agency Co-Applicant Structure**
- Co-applicant agreement must delegate required authorities to co-applicant board
- Agreement must delineate roles and responsibilities of public agency and co-applicant
- Clear division of responsibilities for carrying out Health Center Program project
- Co-applicant board has authority over program-specific decisions

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Governance section, items 3a-c; Form 8: Health Center Agreements; Attachment 2: Bylaws; Attachment 3: Project Organizational Chart; Attachment 6: Co-Applicant Agreement; Attachment 8: Articles of Incorporation (new applicants)

**Notes:**
- Public agencies may use co-applicant structure OR independently meet all governance requirements
- Executive committees cannot have approval/veto power but may act in emergencies

---

**Element b - Required Authorities and Responsibilities**

Articles of incorporation, bylaws, or other documents must outline specific board authorities.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Holding Monthly Meetings**
- Board must meet at least monthly
- Regular schedule established
- Quorum requirements defined
- Meeting procedures documented

**b) Approving Selection and Termination of Project Director/CEO**
- Board has sole authority to select CEO
- Board has sole authority to dismiss/terminate CEO
- Process for selection documented
- Process for termination documented
- CEO accountable to board

**c) Approving Annual Health Center Program Project Budget and Applications**
- Board approves annual budget before submission to HRSA
- Board approves all HRSA applications (competing, non-competing, change in scope)
- Board reviews financial reports
- Board ensures fiscal responsibility

**d) Approving Health Center Services and Site Locations/Hours**
- Board approves types of services offered
- Board approves service delivery sites
- Board approves hours of operation
- Board approves changes to services, sites, or hours

**e) Evaluating Performance of the Health Center**
- Board conducts regular performance evaluations
- Board reviews clinical quality metrics
- Board reviews financial performance
- Board reviews patient satisfaction
- Board reviews achievement of goals and objectives

**f) Establishing or Adopting Policy Related to Operations**
- Board establishes policies governing health center operations
- Board approves major operational policies
- Staff implements policies (board establishes, staff executes)
- Regular policy review and updates

**g) Assuring Compliance with Federal, State, and Local Laws/Regulations**
- Board ensures health center operates in compliance with applicable laws
- Board oversight of regulatory compliance
- Board receives compliance reports
- Board addresses compliance issues

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Governance section, items 2a-g; Attachment 2: Bylaws; Attachment 6: Co-Applicant Agreement

**Notes:**
- Board establishes/approves policies; staff implements and ensures adherence through operating procedures
- All seven authorities must be documented in bylaws or governing documents

## 9. Board Composition (Chapter 20)

### Authority
Section 330(k)(3)(H) of the PHS Act; and 42 CFR 51c.304 and 42 CFR 56.304

### Key Requirements

**Element a - Board Member Selection and Removal Process**

Bylaws must specify processes for ongoing selection and removal of board members, with board control.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Selection Process**
- Bylaws specify how board members are selected
- Process for identifying potential candidates
- Nomination procedures
- Voting/approval procedures
- Term lengths and limits

**b) Removal Process**
- Bylaws specify grounds for removal
- Process for removing board members
- Due process protections
- Voting requirements for removal

**c) Board Control of Selection**
- No other entity can select the board chair
- No other entity can select the majority of board members
- No other entity can select the majority of non-patient board members
- Board retains control over its own composition

**d) Exception for Organizational Representatives**
- Outside entity may remove a board member ONLY if that member was selected by that entity as an organizational representative
- Limited to specific organizational representative seats

**Application Location:** Attachment 2: Bylaws

**Notes:**
- "Board member" refers only to voting members
- Agreements with other organizations cannot permit them to select chair or majority of board

---

**Element b - Required Board Composition**

Bylaws must require the board to be composed according to specific requirements.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Board Size**
- At least 9 members minimum
- No more than 25 members maximum
- Bylaws must prescribe either:
  - A specific number (e.g., "15 members"), OR
  - A range (e.g., "between 12 and 18 members")

**b) Patient Majority (At Least 51%)**
- At least 51% of voting board members must be patients
- Patient definition: Individual who received at least one service in past 24 months that:
  - Generated a health center visit, AND
  - Service and site are within HRSA-approved scope of project
- Legal guardians, health care decision-makers, and legal sponsors may count as patients
- Students who are patients may serve (subject to state laws)

**c) Patient Members Represent Served Population Demographics**
- Patient board members, as a group, represent individuals served
- Representation in terms of:
  - Race
  - Ethnicity
  - Gender
- Consistent with demographics of patient population

**d) Non-Patient Members Representative of Community**
- Non-patient members represent the community served or service area
- May live in the community
- May work in the community
- May have demonstrable connection to the community

**e) Non-Patient Members Provide Relevant Expertise**
- Selected for skills and expertise such as:
  - Community affairs
  - Local government
  - Finance and banking
  - Legal affairs
  - Trade unions
  - Other commercial and industrial concerns
  - Social services
- Diverse skill sets across non-patient members

**f) Health Care Industry Income Limit**
- No more than one-half (50%) of non-patient board members may derive more than 10% of their annual income from health care industry
- Example: 9-member board with 5 patients and 4 non-patients → max 2 non-patients can earn >10% from health care

**g) Employee Exclusion**
- Health center employees cannot be board members
- Immediate family members of employees cannot be board members
- Immediate family defined as: spouses, children, parents, siblings (by blood, adoption, or marriage)
- "Employee" includes:
  - Common-law employees (IRS criteria)
  - Statutory employees (IRS criteria)
  - State/local law employees
- For public agencies with co-applicants: Includes employees of both co-applicant and public agency component

**Application Location:** Project Narrative - Governance section, items 1a-g; Attachment 2: Bylaws; Attachment 6: Co-Applicant Agreement

---

**Element c - Current Board Composition**

Documentation must show the board currently meets all composition requirements.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Current Size**
- At least 9 members
- No more than 25 members
- Documented in Form 6A

**b) Current Patient Majority**
- At least 51% of current board members are patients
- Patient status verified (service in past 24 months)

**c) Patient Demographics Match Served Population**
- Patient board member demographics (race, ethnicity, gender) consistent with:
  - UDS report demographics (for existing health centers), OR
  - Application demographic data (for new applicants)

**d) Special Population Representation**
- For health centers with 330(g), (h), or (i) designation:
  - Representative(s) from or for each special population
  - May be patient board members or advocates
  - Advocates count as "patient" only if they meet patient definition

**e) Non-Patient Member Community Representation**
- Non-patient members are representative of community by:
  - Living in the community, OR
  - Working in the community, OR
  - Having demonstrable connection to community

**f) Non-Patient Member Expertise**
- Non-patient members have relevant skills in areas such as:
  - Community affairs, local government, finance/banking, legal affairs, trade unions, commercial/industrial concerns, social services

**g) Current Health Care Industry Income Compliance**
- No more than 50% of non-patient board members earn more than 10% of annual income from health care industry
- Documented in Form 6A

**Application Location:** Form 6A: Current Board Member Characteristics

---

**Element e - Waiver Requests**

For eligible health centers, waiver of patient majority requirement must demonstrate good cause and alternative strategies.

#### Sub-Requirements:

**a) Eligibility**
- Health center receives award/designation under section 330(g) (Migrant Health), 330(h) (Homeless), and/or 330(i) (Public Housing)
- Health center does NOT receive award/designation under section 330(e) (Community Health Centers)

**b) Good Cause Documentation**

Must document:
- **Unique characteristics** of the special population or service area that create undue hardship in recruiting patient majority
- Examples: High transience, language barriers, distrust of institutions, geographic dispersion
- **Recruitment attempts** within past 3 years:
  - Specific efforts made to recruit special population board members
  - Outreach methods used
  - Barriers encountered
  - Documentation of attempts

**c) Alternative Patient Participation Strategies**

Must address all of the following:

*Collection and Documentation of Input:*
- Methods for collecting input from special population(s)
- Surveys, focus groups, advisory committees, community meetings
- Regular and ongoing collection process

*Communication to Board:*
- Special population input communicated directly to governing board
- Regular reporting mechanism
- Board receives and reviews input

*Incorporation into Key Areas:*
- Selecting health center services
- Setting hours of operation of health center sites
- Defining budget priorities
- Evaluating organization's progress in meeting goals (including patient satisfaction)
- Assessing effectiveness of sliding fee discount program

**Application Location:** Form 6B: Request for Waiver of Board Member Requirements

**Notes:**
- Not applicable if organization is not eligible or has not requested waiver
- Waiver is for patient majority requirement only
- Other composition requirements still apply
